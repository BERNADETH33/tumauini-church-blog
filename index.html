The Red-Brick Psalm

Saint Matthias Parish Church, Tumauini, Isabela

A National Cultural Treasure

Located proudly in the town plaza of Tumauini, Isabela, the Saint Matthias Parish Church is one of the Philippines' most unique and celebrated Spanish-era churches. It is instantly recognizable for two distinguishing features: its all-brick construction and its rare cylindrical bell tower.

This church stands not just as a landmark, but as a lasting symbol of enduring faith, local artistry, and colonial heritage in the Cagayan Valley region.

History: Built to Last

Date / Status Detail
1707 Originally built from light, perishable materials by Dominican friar Francisco Núñez.
1783–1805 Construction on the current, permanent brick structure was started by Fr. Domingo Forto and completed around 1805. It was built with assistance from Pampanga artisans.
World War II The structure was unfortunately damaged during the war, but was later restored with careful conservation efforts.
1989 & Present Declared a National Historical Landmark by the National Historical Institute (NHI) and later recognized as a National Cultural Treasure by the National Museum of the Philippines.

Architectural Highlights: Brick Baroque Masterpiece

All-Brick Design

This is its most defining feature. The church is built almost entirely of baked red bricks—a rare and striking material choice among Philippine colonial churches, which more often used stone or coral.

Detailed Ornamentation

The façade is a tapestry of intricate brick carvings. Look closely to find delicate motifs of flowers, cherubs, and religious patterns decorating the entrance and wall surfaces.

The Cylindrical Bell Tower

Standing separate from the main church, the belfry is the only known circular bell tower from the Spanish colonial era in the country. Its tiered design gives it a unique, stacked appearance, often likened to a wedding cake.

Interior Ambiance

Inside, the warm light streaming through the arched windows, combined with the aged wooden pews and the echoes of history, creates a peaceful and deeply spiritual atmosphere.

Experience & Essence

Walking through the Tumauini Church feels genuinely like traveling through time. You can trace the hand of the 18th-century craftsmen in the detailed brickwork and feel the weight of centuries of worship. It beautifully reflects both the endurance of faith in the region and the artistry of local builders guided by Dominican friars.

Saint Matthias Parish Church is not just a historic landmark—it’s a masterpiece of brick-baroque architecture and a profound testament to enduring devotion.
